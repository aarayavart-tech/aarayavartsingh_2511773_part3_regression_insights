# Final Business Recommendation

## Executive Summary

The objective of this analysis was to identify the factors most strongly associated with monthly sales performance across retail stores using regression analysis.

Based on the regression results, the multiple regression model was selected as the final model because it provided the highest explanatory power and the most useful business insights.

---

## Factors Most Strongly Associated with Monthly Sales

The analysis indicates that the following variables have the strongest positive association with monthly sales:

### 1. Footfall

Footfall emerged as the strongest predictor of sales performance.

Stores with higher customer traffic consistently generated higher monthly sales.

### 2. Marketing Spend

Marketing investment showed a positive relationship with sales.

Higher marketing spend is associated with increased customer awareness and improved store performance.

### 3. Inventory Availability Percentage

Stores maintaining higher inventory availability generally achieved stronger sales results.

Reducing stock shortages appears important for maximizing revenue opportunities.

---

## Variables Leadership Should Prioritize

Based on the regression evidence, leadership should focus on:

### Increasing Customer Traffic

Recommended actions:

* Local promotional campaigns
* Loyalty programs
* Community engagement initiatives
* Digital marketing campaigns

### Improving Inventory Availability

Recommended actions:

* Better demand forecasting
* Improved replenishment planning
* Monitoring stockout rates
* Strengthening supply chain processes

### Optimizing Marketing Investment

Recommended actions:

* Focus spending on high-performing channels
* Measure campaign effectiveness regularly
* Reallocate budgets toward stores with growth potential

---

## Variables That Should Not Be Over-Interpreted

Some variables showed weaker statistical influence or are difficult to interpret independently.

Examples include:

* Store type differences
* Regional differences
* Variables with higher p-values

These variables may reflect broader business conditions rather than direct sales drivers.

---

## Recommended Business Actions

The leadership team should consider the following actions:

1. Increase efforts to drive store traffic.
2. Maintain high inventory availability levels.
3. Continue investing in effective marketing initiatives.
4. Investigate stores with unusually high or low residuals.
5. Develop store-specific improvement plans based on local conditions.

---

## Risks and Limitations

Several limitations should be considered when interpreting the results:

* The analysis uses historical data.
* External economic conditions were not included.
* Customer demographics were not included.
* Competitor actions may affect sales but are not fully captured.
* Store management quality may influence performance.

As a result, the model should be used as a decision-support tool rather than a perfect forecasting system.

---

## Association Does Not Mean Causation

Regression analysis identifies statistical relationships between variables.

However, a positive relationship does not automatically prove that one variable directly causes changes in sales.

For example:

* Higher marketing spend may be associated with higher sales.
* This does not guarantee that increasing marketing alone will always produce proportional sales growth.

Management decisions should combine regression findings with business experience, operational knowledge, and additional testing where possible.

---

## Final Recommendation

The multiple regression model should be adopted as the primary analytical model for sales planning and decision-making.

The strongest business focus should be placed on:

* Increasing footfall
* Improving inventory availability
* Optimizing marketing investments

These factors demonstrate the strongest association with monthly sales performance and provide the greatest opportunity for business improvement.
